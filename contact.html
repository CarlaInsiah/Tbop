Contacts for Support

If you or someone you know is at risk of harm, contact the emergency services in your area!

Here you can find contacts and resources that may be useful if you are looking for support, need someone to talk to or struggling with suicidal or negative thoughts.
Remember, you are not as alone as you might feel.

Samaritans offers free support to anyone who needs to talk or is struggling with suicidal thoughts, "No judgement, no pressure".
Or why not give them a call on: 116 123. It's free!

Mind is a mental health charity that offers free support and advice on various areas of mental health, including crisis coping tools.

Papyrus is the national charity dedicated to suicide prevention in young people under 35 in the UK.

Childline is a support service for anyone under 19 in the UK. They offer conidential help and advice and can be reached online and by phone on 08001111, even late at night.

Campaign Against Living Miserably is a support service specifically targeted towards the prevention of male suicide in the UK.
They offer advice and support for a wide variety of issues, and can be contacted between 5pm and midnight every day.

Woman's Trust offers support and counselling for women suffering or recovering from domestic abuse in the UK.

The NHS Website provides resources and advice for those struggling with suicidal thoughts.

The NHS Website also provides resources and advice for those struggling with domestic violence and abuse, many of which are different from those listed here.

Calm is a wellbeing app that provides support for anxiety and stress, offering meditational and sleep support, promoting positive wellbeing and mental health.
Check them out for some peace of mind.
